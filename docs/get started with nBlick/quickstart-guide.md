# Step by Step Beginners Guide

This guide shows you how to create your first analysis in nBlick.

An analysis is the foundation of your AI visibility tracking.  
It tells nBlick:

- Who you are  
- What market you are in  
- Who your competitors are  
- Which audience you target  

Let’s set it up.

## Step 1: Create a New Analysis

Go to the **Analyses** section.

Click **Create new analysis**.

You will see a setup form with several sections.

Each section helps nBlick understand your business.

## Step 2: Fill in Analysis Information

### Analysis Name

Choose a clear name.

Example:

- `Black Friday Performance - Nike`
- `Q1 AI Visibility - Brand X`

This helps you stay organized if you create multiple analyses.

## Step 3: Add Brand Information

This tells nBlick who you are.

| Field | What to Enter |
|--------|--------------|
| **Brand Name** | Your company or brand name |
| **Website** | Your main domain (example: https://yourbrand.com) |

nBlick uses this to detect when your brand appears in AI answers.

> 💡 Make sure your website is correct. This is how visibility is measured.

## Step 4: Define Your Market

This section explains your business environment.

### Activity Sector

Select your main industry.

Example:
- Ecommerce  
- SaaS  
- Fintech  
- Healthcare  

### Categories (Max 5)

Choose up to 5 categories that describe your business.

These categories are important because:

- They define your tracked prompts
- They shape your content generation
- They influence AI visibility measurement

### Products (Optional)

You can list up to 10 products.

This helps nBlick track product-level visibility.

## Step 5: Set Your Focus Area

This section defines what you want to analyze.

### Topic

Choose the main topic of your analysis.

This could be:

- A product category  
- A campaign  
- A market segment  
- A strategic keyword  

### Competitors (Optional)

Add up to 10 competitors.

This allows nBlick to:

- Compare visibility
- Track competitor mentions
- Analyze citation overlap

> 💡 Adding competitors gives you deeper insights.

## Step 6: Set Audience Context

This tells nBlick which market to simulate.

### Country

Select the country where your customers are located.

AI answers may vary by country.

### Language

Choose the language of analysis.

AI answers are language-dependent.  
Always select the language your customers use.

## Step 7: Create the Analysis

Once all fields are completed:

Click **Create analysis**.

nBlick will:

- Generate prompt categories
- Create tracking prompts
- Start analyzing AI answers
- Monitor citations
- Measure brand visibility

Your dashboard will then populate with:

- Prompts  
- AI answers  
- Citations  
- Visibility data  

# What Happens Next?

After creating your analysis, you can:

1. Review your generated prompts  
2. Track your AI visibility  
3. Analyze competitor presence  
4. Generate optimized content  
5. Improve your AI search performance  

# Best Practices

- Be specific when selecting categories  
- Add real competitors (not just big brands)  
- Choose the correct country and language  
- Keep analysis names organized  

Start focused. You can always create additional analyses later.

# Summary

To create an analysis:

1. Add your brand information  
2. Define your market  
3. Select a focus topic  
4. Add competitors  
5. Choose country and language  
6. Click **Create analysis**

That’s it.

You are now ready to track and improve your AI visibility.
